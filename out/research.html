Research Literature

Crime Mapping, GIS, and Predictive Models

Ahmad et al. (2024). CHART: Intelligent Crime Hotspot Detection and Real-Time Tracking Using Machine Learning. Tech Science Press.

Introduces a machine learning model (CHART) for real-time hotspot tracking using smart preprocessing methods.

Cesario et al. (2024). Multi-density crime predictor: An approach to forecast criminal activities in multi-density crime hotspots. Journal of Big Data.

Combines advanced clustering and time series forecasting to improve hotspot prediction in cities like Chicago.

Kedia (2016). Crime mapping and analysis using GIS. ResearchGate.

Discusses the use of GIS and Kernel Density Estimation (KDE) for spatial crime analysis and visualization.

Levy et al. (2020). SafeRoute: Learning to Navigate Streets Safely in an Urban Environment. University of California.

Proposes a deep reinforcement learning (RL) model that reroutes users away from crime-prone areas.

Towers et al. (2018). Factors influencing temporal patterns in crime in a large American city: A predictive analytics perspective. Purdue University.

Explores how time-based variables (weather, school breaks, paydays) influence crime trends using predictive analytics.

Muthamizharasan & Ponnusamy (2024). A comparative study of crime event forecasting using ARIMA versus LSTM. Journal of Theoretical and Applied Information Technology.

Compares traditional ARIMA and deep learning LSTM models for forecasting crime rates.

Newton et al. (2021). Introduction to the special issue: Urban mobility and crime patterns. European Journal on Criminal Policy and Research.

Discusses the link between urban movement patterns and crime, and introduces the idea of 'dynamic denominators.'

"Mapping the Way to Safer Urban Mobility." EMBARQ Networks.

Explores safe city design and mobility-focused crime risk mitigation strategies, potentially relevant for routing tools.

Race, Policing, and Societal Perceptions

Jefferson (2017). Predictable Policing: Predictive Crime Mapping and Geographies of Policing and Race. American Association of Geographers.

Discusses how race and neighborhood demographics shape crime mapping and raises concerns of bias in predictive systems.

Borg & Hermann (2023). Attitudes toward crime(s) and their relations to gender, age, and personal values.

Analyzes how personal demographics influence opinions about different crimes and safety priorities.

Fear of Crime and Psychological/Social Impacts

Lorenc et al. (2013). Fear of crime and the environment: Systematic review of UK qualitative evidence. BMC Public Health.

A comprehensive review of how fear of crime affects daily life, especially physical activity and mental stress.

George (2010). Visitor perceptions of crime-safety and attitudes towards risk: The case of Table Mountain National Park, Cape Town. Tourism Management.

Shows how crime perception influences tourism and site reputation.

Saad (2023). Personal Safety Fears at Three-Decade High in the U.S. Gallup.

U.S. poll showing rising fear of crime and its effect on public behavior and policy.

Baumer & Rosenbaum (1983). Measuring Fear of Crime: Perceptual, Affective, and Behavioral Components. National Criminal Justice Reference Service.

Classic study breaking down fear into measurable components, showing how fear affects decision-making and behavior.

Crime Severity Perception and Public Opinion

O’Connel & Whelan (1996). Taking wrongs seriously: Public perceptions of crime seriousness. British Journal of Criminology.

Analyzes how the public rates the severity of different crimes (e.g., rape vs. theft), influencing policing priorities.

Wolfgang et al. (1985). National Survey of Crime Severity. Bureau of Justice Statistics.

Large-scale U.S. study ranking crimes based on perceived severity (e.g., murder, assault vs. vandalism).
